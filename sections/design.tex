    In the testbench for the UVM Shift Register, the top-level architecture is shown in Figure 1. Due to the simplicity of the Shift Register design, only one instance of each UVM component is required. The Sequencer and Sequence generate abstract test scenarios, known as transactions, and pass them to the Driver. The current sequence includes approximately 100 randomized multidirectional bit-shifting tests, with randomized control of shift enable, active-low reset, and serial input.

The Interface carries signals to and from the Device Under Test (DUT), allowing communication between the Driver, Monitor, and DUT. The Driver receives abstract transactions from the Sequencer and converts them into pin-level signal activity on the interface.

The Monitor observes DUT interface signals, capturing both the applied stimulus and resulting outputs into transaction objects. These observed transactions are then forwarded to the Scoreboard.

The Scoreboard is the central checking component of the UVM environment. It compares the actual DUT outputs received from the Monitor against the expected outputs generated by the Predictor. It also produces reports indicating whether the Shift Register DUT behaved correctly.

Finally, the Predictor calculates the golden reference outputs based on the transaction inputs and sends the expected results to the Scoreboard.

For future work, this UVM verification environment can be expanded beyond shift registers to support more complex digital designs, such as synchronous and asynchronous FIFOs, counters, FSM-based controllers, and small datapath components. A FIFO would be a natural next step because it introduces important verification challenges, including full and empty conditions, overflow and underflow detection, pointer wraparound, and clock-domain crossing in asynchronous designs.

Additional improvements could include integrating SystemVerilog assertions, directed corner-case tests, and more detailed functional coverage models to strengthen the overall verification quality. These extensions would make the environment more reusable, scalable, and applicable to increasingly complex SoC components in future Purdue SoCET projects.

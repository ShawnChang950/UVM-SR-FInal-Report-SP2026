For future work, this UVM verification environment can be expanded beyond shift registers to more complex digital designs such as synchronous and asynchronous FIFOs, counters, FSM-based controllers, and small datapath components. A FIFO would be a natural next step because it introduces important verification challenges such as full and empty conditions, overflow and underflow detection, pointer wraparound, and clock-domain crossing in asynchronous designs. Additional improvements could include integrating functional coverage, SystemVerilog assertions, and automated regression testing to strengthen verification quality. These extensions would help build a more reusable and scalable UVM framework that can support increasingly complex SoC components in future Purdue SoCET projects.
